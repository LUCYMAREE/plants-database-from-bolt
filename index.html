Discover New Zealand's Unique Biodiversity

Explore the incredible native flora and fauna that make Aotearoa New Zealand one of the world's most unique ecosystems. From ancient forests to alpine meadows, discover the plants and animals that call this land home.

Featured Native Species

Learn about New Zealand's endemic plants and animals, many found nowhere else on Earth.

Pohutukawa

New Zealand Christmas Tree

Iconic coastal tree with vibrant crimson flowers that bloom during summer, earning it the nickname "New Zealand Christmas Tree."

Harakeke

New Zealand Flax

Traditional weaving plant essential to Māori culture, with strong fibers used for clothing, baskets, and rope for over 1000 years.

Silver Fern

Ponga

New Zealand's national symbol, this majestic tree fern grows up to 10 meters tall and displays distinctive silver undersides on its fronds.

Kowhai

Golden Flower Tree

New Zealand's national flower, producing stunning golden-yellow blooms that attract native birds and signal the arrival of spring.

Cabbage Tree

Tī kōuka

Distinctive palm-like native tree with sword-shaped leaves and fragrant white flower clusters, an important food source for early Māori.

Kakapo

Night Parrot

World's only flightless parrot, this nocturnal bird is critically endangered with only around 250 individuals remaining in the wild.

Tui

Parson Bird

Distinctive native honeyeater with iridescent feathers and white throat tufts, known for their complex songs and acrobatic feeding displays.

Tuatara

Living Fossil

Ancient reptile species that has remained virtually unchanged for 200 million years, found only on offshore islands around New Zealand.

Kiwi

National Bird

Flightless nocturnal bird and national symbol of New Zealand, with hair-like feathers and the unique ability to smell with its long beak.

Hector's Dolphin

Māui's Dolphin

World's smallest dolphin species, endemic to New Zealand waters and critically endangered with distinctive rounded dorsal fin.

Weta

Giant Cricket

Large prehistoric insect, some species weighing up to 100 grams, endemic to New Zealand and crucial to native ecosystem health.

Manuka

Tea Tree

Small native tree famous for producing medicinal honey, with small white flowers and aromatic leaves used traditionally for healing.

Conservation & Protection

Our Mission

New Zealand's unique isolation has created extraordinary biodiversity, with 80% of native plants and 90% of insects found nowhere else on Earth. Our mission is to educate and inspire conservation efforts to protect these irreplaceable species.
